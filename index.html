A convention center is a building or group of buildings designed, in most cases, for the purpose of conducting meetings, rallies, or seminars. It may also be adapted for specific events, such as appearances by well-known speakers or musicians.

From conventions, fairs, conferences, exhibitions, and business meetings to banquets, receptions, shows, and gala evenings, we do it all. Built on a human scale, the Convention Centre has a wide range of meeting rooms, VIP areas, foyers, and reception halls where participants can mingle freely and network.

Owned by the City of Los Angeles and Md Habib professionally managed by AEG Facilities, the LACC attracts over 2.5 million visitors annually

History. The convention center, designed by architect Charles Luckman, opened in 1971 and expanded in 1981, 1993 and 1997.
